Margin health

Know your problem products at a glance.

The color-coded margin system makes it immediately obvious which products need attention — without reading every row of a table.

Margin % is calculated as gross profit margin: (Revenue − COGS) ÷ Revenue × 100. Industry standard, not markup.

🔴

Below 20% — Review needed

Likely unprofitable after Shopify fees, payment processing, and shipping. These products need a price increase or cost reduction.

🟡

20–40% — Watch closely

Low but workable. Fees eat into this range significantly. Consider bundling or repositioning.

🟢

40%+ — Healthy margin

Strong gross margin. Enough room to absorb discounts, returns, and shipping without going into the red.

⚪

No cost set — Incomplete data

Cost not entered yet. The app highlights these so you can prioritize adding costs for your top-selling products first.

Common questions.

Do I need to enter all my costs before the app is useful?

No. On install, the app runs a coverage audit and shows you exactly how many products have cost data vs. how many are missing. You can start with your top 10 products by revenue and work from there. The app filters to "missing costs only" to help you prioritize.

If I already have costs in Shopify, do I need to re-enter them?

No. We read your existing cost_per_item data on install. If you've already filled in costs in Shopify Admin → Products → Inventory → Cost per item, they're already in the app on day one.

Does it track ad spend or LTV?

No. This app focuses purely on gross margin — revenue minus the cost of the goods sold. It doesn't track advertising spend, shipping costs, or customer lifetime value. If you need those, BeProfit or Lifetimely are built for that. We're the affordable version that does gross margins exceptionally well.

What happens to historical data if I update a product's cost?

Nothing — historical orders are protected. When an order is placed, we lock the cost at that moment. Future cost changes only affect new orders. Your historical margin data stays accurate.

Does it work with multi-currency stores?

In v1.0, margins are shown in your store's primary currency. Multi-currency support with per-currency margin breakdowns is on the roadmap.
